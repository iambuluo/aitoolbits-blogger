The AI Writing Tool I Use Every Day That Nobody Talks About

By dengf  |  Published: July 15, 2026  |  Category: Behind the Scenes

Everyone argues about ChatGPT vs. Claude vs. Gemini. Meanwhile the tool that quietly saves me the most time isn't a chatbot at all — it's a plain-text snippet expander I built myself. No AI company sells it. It's boring. And it might be the most underrated thing in my whole stack.

What It Actually Does

I type a short trigger like /review and it drops a full, pre-written section skeleton into whatever I'm writing — a product-review template with rating rows, a pros/cons block, a verdict line. Before AI, I kept these in a notes app and copy-pasted. Now they're one keystroke. It sounds trivial. It isn't: I fire these 30+ times a day.

Why This Beats Asking an AI

When I ask a chatbot for a template, I wait for a response, then I edit its wording to match my voice. With the snippet expander, the template is already in my voice because I wrote it once. The AI helps me fill the variable parts; the skeleton is mine. That split — machine for the variable, me for the structure — is where the speed comes from.

The Lesson for Anyone Building a Pipeline

We obsess over which model is smartest and ignore the dumb mechanical steps that eat our day. Auto-expanding templates, saved regex filters, a one-click "strip the AI filler" macro — these save more cumulative time than switching models ever will. The flashy tool gets the blog post; the boring tool gets the work done.

Try It This Week

You don't need to code. Every modern editor (VS Code, Obsidian, even some email clients) has snippet/expansion built in. Write down the three blocks of text you type most often, assign each a short trigger, and never type them fully again. A half hour of setup, a year of saved minutes.
