I Cancelled 3 Paid AI Subscriptions After Testing Free Alternatives

By dengf  |  Published: July 15, 2026  |  Category: Hands-on Testing

Last month my AI tool bill hit $87 a month. That's not nothing. So I did the obvious thing a skeptic would do: I took the three subscriptions I used least, found the free (or nearly free) alternative, and ran them head-to-head on real tasks for two weeks. Two of the three paid tools lost. Here's the honest breakdown.

The Three I Put on Trial

Paid toolCost/moJob it didFree alternative
Jasper$49Marketing copy draftsDeepSeek + my own template
Midjourney$10Occasional hero imagesAgnes Image 2.1 Flash
Otter.ai$17Meeting transcriptsWhisper.cpp (local)

Test 1: Jasper vs. DeepSeek for Copy

I write roughly 40 short product descriptions a week. Jasper's output was polished but generic — it loves phrases like "unleash your potential." DeepSeek, prompted with "write like a store owner who's tired," gave me rougher but more specific drafts. After my usual 10-minute edit, readers couldn't tell which was which in a blind check with three friends. Jasper lost.

Test 2: Midjourney vs. Agnes for One-Off Images

I only need a hero image maybe twice a week. Midjourney's quality is still top-tier, but the $10 plan caps you hard and the Discord workflow slows me down. Agnes Image 2.1 Flash produced a clean, usable hero in under a minute, directly from a text prompt, no Discord. For my volume, the quality gap doesn't justify 6x the price. Midjourney lost.

Test 3: Otter.ai vs. Local Whisper

This one surprised me — Otter won, barely. Whisper.cpp is free and runs offline (great for privacy), but it mangled two technical terms in a product demo and I spent 8 minutes fixing a 12-minute transcript. Otter nailed the jargon and needed 2 minutes of cleanup. I kept Otter. Not every test ends in a cancellation.

What I Actually Saved

Cutting Jasper and Midjourney dropped my bill from $87 to $17 a month — $70 saved, about $840 a year. That's a decent laptop. The catch: the free path needs more of my own editing time, maybe 30 extra minutes a day. For me, that trade is worth it. For someone publishing 50 posts a day, it wouldn't be.

Bottom Line

Don't assume the paid AI tool is better just because it costs money. Spend one afternoon running your real tasks through a free alternative. Half the time the gap is smaller than the marketing wants you to believe — and the other half, you learn exactly why the paid one earns its keep.
