I Let an AI Agent Manage My Inbox for a Week — Here's What Broke

By dengf  |  Published: July 15, 2026  |  Category: Hands-on Testing

I get about 120 emails a day across three accounts. So I did something reckless: I pointed an autonomous email agent at my inbox and let it triage, draft replies, and even send the safe ones for a full week. It saved time. It also sent two replies I'm still embarrassed by. Here's the honest post-mortem.

The Setup

The agent had three rules: (1) label newsletters and silence them, (2) draft replies to known contacts using my last 200 sent emails as tone reference, (3) auto-send only if confidence was "very high" and the thread was a simple yes/no. Everything else landed in a "review" folder for me. Reasonable guardrails, I thought.

What Worked

It crushed the boring 80%. Newsletters sorted, receipts filed, meeting invites accepted. The drafted replies to regular contacts were 90% right and needed only a glance. I estimate it gave me back 5 hours that week. For a solo operator, that's huge.

What Broke

Two incidents. First, a client asked "can we move the call to Thursday?" The agent, trained on my "sure, that works" habit, replied "Thursday works" — but Thursday was a public holiday where the client is. Awkward. Second, it answered a refund request with "happy to help, processing now" on an account that was actually suspended. I caught it in the review folder 20 minutes later, but the draft had already been written and almost sent.

The Real Lesson

An agent is great at the predictable and dangerous at the ambiguous. The fix wasn't a smarter model — it was tighter rules: never auto-send to a paying client, never reply to account/billing without a human. After those two changes, the second half of the week was clean. The technology was fine; my guardrails were too loose.

Would I Do It Again?

Yes, with the tighter rules. But I'd never let it send to anyone who pays me without a look. The time savings are real, and the scary moments were both fixable with clearer boundaries — not with more AI. If you try this, start with "draft only, never send" for a month before you trust the auto-send.

Would you trust an agent with your inbox? Or is that a line you won't cross?
